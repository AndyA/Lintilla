`The face that launched a thousand frocks, according to the Daily Telegraph. She spent two years as a catwalk model for Lagerfeld, Chanel, Armani and Comme des Garcons. So why isnt she on the Style page? Because shes a professional cellist. She paid for her first decent cello with modelling money. The inevitable headline? `Model cellist swaps catwalk for catgut. So has she put her glamour career behind her? Hardly. Philips is marketing her debut CD by selling her on her looks. She follows in the footsteps of Vanessa Mae (she of the wet T- shirt), Ann-Sophie Mutter (she of the legendary cleavage), and Linda Brava (who unfurled herself for Playboy). Kotova, 28 and 5ft 11ins, is the latest pin-up. Brief biog please? Her father Ivan Kotov was the Soviet Unions leading double bass player until he fell foul of Soviet bureaucracy. He dropped tofifth double bass in the state symphony orchestra and was threatened with incarceration in a mental institution. Kotova started playing the cello at the age of seven. At 15 she won the Prague International Competition. After leaving Russia, she studied at Cologne Conservatory and Yale. Now she lives in Dallas with `a special friend. That probably doesnt mean her cello. And her new CD? 14 short romantic pieces performed with the Moscow Chamber Orchestra. Theres Tchaikovsky, Rachmaninov, Elgar and two pieces by the cellist herself, one entitled Sketches from the Catwalk. Any good? `Her playing is of the highest quality, her tone sensuous and round, her interpretation full of passion, vitality and precision, claimed the London Evening Standard. But US critic Edith Eisler argues: `The playing reflects the rather limited expression and mood of the music. And Kotovas verdict? `It goes with Anna Karenina, reading, a fireplace, she says.
